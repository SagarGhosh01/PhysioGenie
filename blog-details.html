    Pelvic Tilt :- Pelvic tilt exercises help strengthen the lower back and core muscles, improving posture and reducing pain. A pelvic tilt occurs when the pelvis tilts forward or backward due to weak core muscles, prolonged sitting, poor posture, or muscle imbalances Knee-to-Chest Stretch:- The knee-to-chest stretch helps relieve lower back pain, improve flexibility, and reduce tension in the hips and spine. Tight lower back muscles, poor posture, prolonged sitting, or spinal issues can cause stiffness, making this stretch beneficial for relaxation and mobility. McKenzie Press-Ups:-McKenzie press-ups are a spine extension exercise that helps relieve pain from a herniated disc by reducing nerve pressure and improving lumbar mobility. Disc bulging, poor posture, or prolonged sitting can cause lower back pain, and this exercise helps restore natural spinal alignment. Cat-Cow Stretch:- The Cat-Cow stretch improves spinal flexibility, relieves back pain, and promotes better posture by alternating between arching and rounding the back. Poor posture, prolonged sitting, or muscle stiffness can lead to spinal discomfort, and this exercise helps keep the spine mobile and healthy. Seated Hamstring Stretch:-The Seated Hamstring Stretch helps improve flexibility, reduce lower back tension, and relieve tight hamstrings caused by prolonged sitting, poor posture, or muscle imbalances. This stretch enhances mobility and prevents strain on the lower back and legs.

    Chin Tucks – Helps improve neck posture by strengthening deep cervical muscles and aligning the head over the shoulders. Poor posture, prolonged screen time, or slouching can lead to forward head syndrome, causing neck pain and stiffness. This exercise reduces strain and enhances spinal alignment. Neck Side Stretch – Relieves stiffness and tension in the neck muscles by stretching the sides of the neck and upper shoulders. It is beneficial for people experiencing tightness due to prolonged sitting, stress, or sleeping in an awkward position. Regular stretching improves flexibility and reduces muscle discomfort. Isometric Neck Exercises – Strengthens neck muscles without actual movement by applying resistance using your hands. These exercises help improve neck stability and reduce pain caused by muscle weakness, poor posture, or whiplash injuries. They also aid in preventing strain from daily activities.

    Wrist Curls – Strengthens the forearm muscles and improves wrist stability, helping with conditions like tendinitis, carpal tunnel syndrome, or weakness after injury. Repetitive strain from daily activities or sports can weaken the wrist, and wrist curls aid in restoring strength and endurance.

    Straight Leg Raises – Strengthens the quadriceps without putting stress on the knee joint, making it ideal for knee rehabilitation. Weak quads can lead to knee instability, often caused by injury, surgery, or arthritis. This exercise helps improve knee control and prevent further strain. Quad Sets – Activates and strengthens the quadriceps by contracting the muscles without moving the knee. This is useful for recovering from ACL injuries, meniscus tears, or post-surgery rehabilitation. It helps maintain muscle function and reduce stiffness in the knee joint. Heel Slides – Improves knee mobility and flexibility by gently bending and straightening the knee. This exercise is beneficial for recovering from surgery, injuries, or arthritis, where knee stiffness and limited range of motion are common issues.
